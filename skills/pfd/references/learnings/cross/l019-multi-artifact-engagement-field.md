# Learning 19: Multi-Artifact Engagement Field and Relay-User Mode

**Insight:** PFD's 5-layer stack assumes (a) ONE artifact at a time and (b) a BUYER directly evaluating that artifact. Real B2B engagements violate both simultaneously: (a) the client experiences a *field* of artifacts (landing page + deck + proposal + emails + docs + call demeanor) as a single composite perception, not a sequence of discrete analyses; and (b) the primary contact is often a *relay* — someone building internal alignment or selling a committee they're trying to convince — not the final decision-maker. Two failure modes result.

**Failure mode 1 — methodology self-contradiction at engagement level.** When only ONE artifact in the field gets PFD treatment (a styled landing page) while the others remain unstyled markdown / plain text / inconsistent formatting, the engagement composite exhibits Learning #15 at a scale no single-artifact PFD analysis can see. A PFD-methodology advocate shipping beautiful landing pages with markdown proposals is experientially self-contradictory even when each artifact individually passes PFD evaluation. The 5-layer stack applied artifact-by-artifact misses the composite signal entirely.

**Failure mode 2 — wrong evaluation criteria for relay users.** When the primary contact is a relay (VP trying to build team alignment, Head of Innovation selling a committee, PM trying to convince a CEO), PFD's standard tests — "does the buyer decide?" — produce the wrong prescriptions. Relay users need artifacts that *arm them rhetorically* for their internal pitch. Every PFD prescription for a relay-user context needs a secondary evaluation pass: "does this give the relay quotable, defensible, forwardable lines?" — not just "does this convert the relay?" A page that converts the relay but contains nothing they can paste into their internal memo is an L4 failure the current framework doesn't detect.

**Derivation protocol addition (candidate for v0.4):**
- Before Step 1 (stating the problem): identify the engagement FIELD. List every artifact in it. Identify which get PFD treatment and which don't. Every un-treated artifact is an L2 processing-fluency violation at the composite level.
- After Step 5 (testing proposals): run the relay-user audit. For each prescription, ask: "If this is forwarded/pasted into a committee memo, does it support the relay's case, contradict it, or sit inert?"

**Current workarounds until formalized:**
- Style all major client-facing artifacts (proposals, decks, one-pagers) with consistent PFD-derived visual hierarchy — including the ones that feel "just internal." Markdown proposals from a PFD practitioner are the Learning #15 smoking gun.
- When designing for a known relay user, explicitly write down what the relay needs to bring to their internal audience. Make prescriptions quotable.

**Generalizes:** Any engagement with 2+ artifacts evaluated by any contact who is not the final decision-maker. Most B2B enterprise sales. Partnership negotiations. Internal change initiatives where the champion is selling a framework to leadership. Any consultancy relationship with multiple deliverables.

**Connection to other learnings:**
- **Extends Learning #15** (experiential self-contradiction) from artifact-level to engagement-level — when one artifact's polish contradicts another artifact's rawness, the composite signals methodology-incoherence even if each piece evaluates well individually.
- **Extends Learning #4** (workspace vs product separation) from within-product to across-artifact — the "fix this artifact" vs "fix the field" boundary is the analog of the "fix this workspace" vs "fix the product" boundary.
- **Extends Learning #3** (pre-send vs post-response) to "pre-handoff vs post-handoff" — what the relay takes with them into internal meetings is higher-leverage than what happens in the original reading session.
- **Bumps against Learning #9** (temporal/session continuity) — both are "missing PFD dimensions." Learning #9 is time within-artifact; Learning #19 is space across-artifacts. A complete v0.4 L5/L6 formalization should treat these as the paired temporal + spatial extensions to the current 5-layer spatial-within-moment stack.

**Caveat:** Single-engagement observation. Validation needed on 2-3 more diverse B2B engagements (product pitches, client stakeholder reviews, agency leads) before committing to layer-level formalization. Until then: use as practitioner heuristic via the protocol additions above.

**Framework implication:** Candidate for formalization as an L5 layer (multi-artifact composite) OR as a derivation-protocol augmentation in v0.4.0. Not promoted to layer-level yet; first, verify the pattern holds across independent engagements rather than being a single-client observation.
