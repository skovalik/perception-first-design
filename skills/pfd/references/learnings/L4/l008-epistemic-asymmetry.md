# Learning 8: Epistemic Asymmetry as L4 Problem

**Insight:** When an AI system gathers context via invisible mechanisms (BFS graph traversal, connection strength priorities, depth limits), the resulting epistemic asymmetry (AI knows more than user about its own context) is a Layer 3 violation. Users' System 1 response to opaque AI behavior is distrust. The gap between what they feel and what they can articulate is exactly where PFD Layer 3 operates. The intervention is making the mechanism visible pre-send, not explaining it post-response. Generalizes to any expert/client asymmetry: show your reasoning process, not just your conclusions.
