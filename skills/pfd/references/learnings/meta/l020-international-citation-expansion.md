# Learning 20: International Citation Expansion Surfaced Pre-Verbal Arousal Backbone and Cultural Calibration Meta-Rule

**Insight:** PFD's published-study base was ~80% Western before this pass. Three substantive findings emerged that would have stayed invisible without the international lens.

**(1) Pre-verbal arousal has a broader empirical backbone than originally cited.** The original L3 autopilot section cited Nisbett & Wilson 1977 and Kahneman 2011. The international pass surfaced Hassin 2013 "Yes It Can" review (unconscious performs every high-level cognitive function previously assumed to require awareness), Pessiglione et al. 2007 (subliminal incentive processing drives motor behavior), and Mori 1970 (uncanny valley as negative-polarity pre-verbal response). Pre-verbal arousal becomes a complete empirical object with positive and negative polarities, dual-task evidence, and fMRI validation. Not just "System 1 before System 2."

**(2) Cultural Calibration promotes from open question to working meta-rule.** The existing "Cultural generalizability" open question asked "how does the stack hold in non-WEIRD markets?" The cross-cultural literature (Masuda/Nisbett 2001, Chua 2005, Goh 2010, Ji 2001) resolves part of it: *the architecture is universal; the calibration is cultural*. L1 parameters (what triggers trust) and L3 parameters (predictive priors) must be validated against target-market users before cross-regional rollout. This is now a working rule, not an unresolved caveat. What remains open: L2 across non-Latin typographic traditions, L4 across high-context vs low-context cultures, dependency-stack ordering invariance.

**(3) Methodology Siblings is a framework-level addition, not a scholarly footnote.** Kansei engineering (Nagamachi 1995, Japan), Gestalt psychology (Wertheimer 1923 / Metzger 1936, Berlin), and neuroaesthetics (Skov & Nadal 2020; Leder et al. 2004) each solve the same problem PFD solves, from different angles. Naming them explicitly licenses PFD into their tradition and signals intellectual heritage for Japanese and European audiences. It also sharpens what PFD uniquely adds: the ordering rule (dependency stack) and the operational protocol.

**Practical implication 1 (research methodology).** Before citing a framework as "well-supported," audit for geographic bias. A 10-country pass before v3.6 would have saved this as a native feature rather than a patch. International citation audits should be a pre-release gate, not a post-release patch.

**Practical implication 2 (client engagement as R&D).** The prospect's Design Rationale drove this work. A European enterprise prospect required cross-tradition citations to license PFD at their stakeholder level. The client conversation revealed a methodology gap that internal audits had not surfaced. Client-specific requirements expose invisible framework gaps that self-review misses.

**Practical implication 3 (confidence calibration).** PFD v3.5 claimed 82 citations. That number was true of citations cited, but misleading about geographic/linguistic coverage. The claim "psychology-backed design framework" is stronger at ~100 international cites than at 82 Western cites, even though the underlying mechanisms are the same. Honest counting requires more than counting.

**Connection to other learnings:**
- **Extends Learning #7 (convergent gap identification across rounds).** Six parallel research agents, each tuned to a different linguistic tradition, returned the same three structural gaps (pre-verbal arousal breadth, cultural calibration, methodology heritage). That convergence was the validation signal to promote these additions to framework level rather than footnote.
- **Extends Learning #19 (multi-artifact engagement field).** The prospect's engagement field (proposal + Design Rationale + supporting docs) drove the framework-level change. The single-artifact view (the proposal) wouldn't have surfaced this. The composite view (the full client engagement) did.
- **Connects to Learning #14 (operational vs structural ethics).** PFD's operational ethics (three tests) assume good practitioner values. The structural ethics (business model, client filtering) do not prevent geographic bias in the framework's knowledge base. A scholarly-bias audit is a new structural ethics mechanism: what the framework does not see because of where its sources come from.
