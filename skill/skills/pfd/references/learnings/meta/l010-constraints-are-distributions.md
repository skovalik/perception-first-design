# Learning 10: Constraints Are Distributions, Not Constants

**Insight:** PFD states working memory holds "~3-5 chunks" as a hard constraint. This is a population mean. Neurodivergent users, users under cognitive load, users with fatigue all shift the distribution. The constraint is real but the threshold varies. PFD should note that its constraints define the shape of the curve, not a fixed cutoff — good design provides calibration mechanisms for users at the tails. This doesn't weaken PFD — it strengthens it by acknowledging that perception science describes populations, not individuals.

**2026-04-25 update — Score-Band Tri-Cluster Empirical Refinement (PFD Calibration & Showcase Campaign):** Running the rubric at scale (n=25 Fortune-500 sweep) surfaced an empirical property of the PFD score distribution that refines this learning's "constraints are distributions" framing for the rubric itself. The 25-episode score distribution self-organized into three discrete bands — Catastrophic/Failing (27–47, 11 episodes: Amazon Prime 27, AWS 35, Xfinity 40, DoorDash 40, Reddit 41, IKEA 42, Booking 42, United 44, LinkedIn 44, Salesforce 46, Spirit 47), Partial/Moderate (48–62, 10 episodes), and Strong/Polish (70–80, 3 episodes plus Wikipedia at 80 as joint ceiling). Notably, **no episodes scored in 63–69**. The 8-point gap is statistically suspicious enough to suggest the rubric has a real perceptual *threshold* near 63 where a property either has named-pattern accumulation or doesn't — meaning PFD scores aren't continuously distributed, they're tri-modal. The likely mechanism: a design either has *no* named patterns (lands in 70+ band), *one or two* compounding patterns (48–62 band), or *three or more* stacking patterns (27–47 band) — and the band boundaries reflect qualitative state-change thresholds rather than continuous gradient. The framework implication: this learning's "constraints are distributions" framing applies recursively to the rubric — the score is itself a distribution with structure, not a continuous gradient. Practical use for client triage: a 55 is qualitatively different from a 45 (one named pattern vs three); the gap > 8 between band ceiling and next band floor means small score deltas across a band boundary indicate qualitative regime change, not incremental difference. Validate on additional corpus runs before treating the tri-modal property as fully established beyond the n=25 calibration sample.
