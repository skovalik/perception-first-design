# Learning 16: Near-Miss Color Asymmetry — Small Deviations Cost More Than Large Departures

**Insight:** Human color perception is non-additive: large color differences are perceived as *less* than the sum of their small-step components (Bujack et al., 2022 — "diminishing returns"). This means the perceptual cost curve is non-linear. A brand blue that's 3% off-target imposes *disproportionate* processing cost compared to a completely different color, because small deviations sit in the steep, high-sensitivity zone of the perceptual metric where prediction error is maximal. PFD's L2 heuristic "3-4 colors, consistent" is directionally correct but operates at the wrong level of abstraction: "consistent" in hex/RGB space ≠ consistent in perceptual space. Every CSS gradient, palette interpolation, and color scale built linear in hex is non-linear in perception — a systematic L2 fluency violation invisible to the designer. **Practical implication:** near-miss color deviations (slightly off-brand, almost-but-not-quite matching) are the highest-cost L2 violations, more disruptive than using a completely different color. Design-token linting that flags off-system values should weight near-misses as more severe than far-misses. **Framework connection:** the Feb 2026 Los Alamos finding (Bujack et al., 2025, *Computer Graphics Forum*) that hue, saturation, and lightness are intrinsic geometric properties of the color metric — not learned or cultural — confirms PFD's hardware/software distinction for the color channel specifically. Color *structure* is hardware (universal geometry). Color *meaning* is software (cultural, context-dependent). **Caveat:** the non-additivity finding rests on aggregate data collected online across subjects; Brainard (2022, PNAS commentary — "Proximity Matters") flags that aggregate non-Riemannian behavior could be a statistical artifact of averaging individually Riemannian observers with different parameters (cone ratios, lens yellowing). The design implication holds regardless — near-misses are costly whether the mechanism is geometric or statistical — but the theoretical claim ("color space is non-Riemannian") should be cited as a theoretical framework, not settled law. The Feb 2026 "Schrödinger's theory completed" headlines overstate an incremental mathematical contribution (formalizing one missing component, the neutral axis). Cite the non-additivity finding cautiously; don't cite the "completed" framing.

**2026-04-25 update — Tri-Axis Expansion (PFD Calibration & Showcase Campaign):** Running color audits across the n=25 sweep surfaced two additional axes that complement the original near-miss-asymmetry framing into a complete L1/L2 color-discipline primitive. **(1) Pre-attentive priority** (Treisman & Gelade 1980; Wolfe 1994) — color is processed before shape or text in the perception pipeline, which means color delivers its meaning *before* any label can correct it. The Spirit ep 03 fix — inverting confirm-affordance color and "fixing it with a label" — is not a fix at the perceptual level; the label arrives too late in the pipeline. The sub-rule: when affordance color is wrong, the label cannot rescue it. **(2) Brand-prior reassignment** — the worst position on the near-miss curve is not just any near-miss, it is the brand's *load-bearing* color reassigned to a paid or extractive surface. United ep 04 reassigns United Blue (the brand's strongest affordance color) to a seat-map upcharge, producing dysfluency the user attributes to "feels greedy" rather than "the colors are confusing" because the predictive prior insists "this is the brand" while the function has inverted. The diagnostic question this adds: "What is the brand's load-bearing color, and where is it being reassigned?" **(3) Iso-styling / equality-as-obstruction** — when competing affordances render at identical visual weight (Amazon Iliad ep 07's three iso-styled buttons "Remind Me Later · Keep My Membership · Cancel My Benefits"; AWS Console Home ep 08's 8 widgets at equal weight; Reddit modern card variants ep 11), the 50ms gestalt verdict (Lindgaard 2006) literally cannot locate the user's intended action — the equality of weights *is* the obstruction. This is a third L1/L2 mechanism distinct from inversion (the original #16 framing) and density (#11): equality-as-noise. The complete L2 color-discipline axiom now reads as a tri-axis primitive: color is read first (pre-attentive priority) AND its costs are asymmetric (near-miss cost curve) AND the worst case is brand-prior reassignment, with iso-styling-as-obstruction as the limiting case where equal weights produce no signal at all. **(4) Per-card color-signal saturation** — quantifies a saturation threshold the original framing did not name. When a single component carries 5+ reserved-meaning colors simultaneously (Booking ep 22's card with red scarcity + coral discount + yellow urgency + green sustainability + navy review + yellow CTA), each color's reserved meaning collapses and System 1 reads the card as "loud" — the design system has lost its color-as-signal lever entirely. Practical rule: count reserved colors per card; >3 reserved roles per surface produces saturation collapse per the Reber & Schwarz fluency threshold. The campaign's evidence on this expansion is 4+ episodes (Spirit, United, Reddit, Booking explicit; Adobe + Amazon brand-color fixes implicit); generalize on additional palette-design audits before treating the tri-axis as exhaustive.
