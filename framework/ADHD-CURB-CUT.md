# ADHD and the Curb-Cut Effect

The cognitive constraints I design around are constraints every user has. Mine are just louder.

## The Diagnostic Instrument

I'm autistic. After my formal ASD diagnosis, I realized I'd been doing something most designers do intuitively but can't explain: learning social cognition analytically. I didn't absorb what a pause signals or what "said vs. unsaid" reveals through instinct. I learned it like an instrument, through theory and practice loops. That analytical process is what lets me see and articulate the unconscious patterns driving user behavior.

I also have severe combined-type ADHD. Some experiences are literally painful for me because of how taxing they are on my attention and cognitive load. Friction, clutter, noise, confusion: the things bad design inflicts on everyone, I feel at a higher volume. What most designers theorize about, I feel.

That sensitivity is a diagnostic instrument. When I arrive at a page, the emotional response fires before language catches up. System 1 before System 2. My ADHD sensitivity means I feel cognitive load and disfluency more acutely than most users. The feeling IS the diagnostic. The autism means I can then unpack exactly why, tracing the violation back to the systematic rule that's broken.

## The Curb-Cut Effect

Cowan (2010) established that working memory holds 3-5 chunks. ADHD brains hit the ceiling of that range faster and harder, making violations visible sooner. A page that overloads an ADHD user's working memory is overloading everyone's. The ADHD user just notices first.

This is the curb-cut effect applied to cognition. Curb cuts were designed for wheelchair users but turned out to help everyone: parents with strollers, travelers with luggage, delivery workers with carts. Designs that reduce cognitive load for ADHD users reduce cognitive load for all users. The constrained case reveals what the general case tolerates but shouldn't.

The research supports this directly. Kasatskii et al. (JetBrains, 2023) found that high perceptual load degrades ADHD-symptomatic developers' performance faster than neurotypical developers'. Mark et al. (2008) measured context-switching recovery at 23 minutes for typical workers. For ADHD developers, Mark et al. (2024) found the cost approaches an hour. The same friction, amplified. Design for the amplified case and the typical case improves too.

## Cognitive Constraint Design

The Inverse Inflection research project surfaced a unifying meta-framework: Cognitive Constraint Design. The principle is that cognitive constraints are design material, not obstacles to work around.

| Domain | Constraint | Design Response |
|--------|-----------|-----------------|
| PFD (interfaces) | Limited processing capacity | Reduce cognitive load, optimize fluency |
| ADHD-AI workflows | Executive dysfunction | Externalize structure at point of performance |
| Spatial workflow tools | Working memory limits | Spatial triggers externalize planning |
| AI writing assistants | Communication pattern drift | Corrections-as-training preserves authentic voice |

Same principle, different surfaces. PFD applies Cognitive Constraint Design to interfaces. ADHD-AI workflows apply it to creative process. Spatial tools apply it to working memory. The methodology and its applications are expressions of one idea: build for the constraint, and the unconstrained case gets better.

## The Dual Foundation

This framework draws from 77 citations across two non-overlapping literatures: 55 from perception science (processing fluency, predictive processing, cognitive load theory, visual attention) and 22 from ADHD cognitive neuroscience (executive function, divergent thinking, interest-based attention, environmental fit). Zero overlap between the two bodies. Bridging them into a single actionable design framework is the novel contribution.

## Citation Highlights

Key ADHD citations from the framework (full set in Appendix A of the [complete reference](PERCEPTION-FIRST-DESIGN.md)):

- **Barkley (1997):** ADHD as deficit in behavioral inhibition and executive function, not attention per se.
- **Castellanos et al. (2002):** Longitudinal MRI showing ADHD-associated differences in cerebral volume.
- **White & Shah (2006, 2011):** ADHD adults outperform on divergent thinking, underperform on convergent. More original, more unusual associations.
- **Carson, Peterson & Higgins (2003):** Eminent creative achievers 7x more likely to have low latent inhibition. Failure to filter irrelevant stimuli enables novel associations.
- **Dodson (2016):** Interest-based nervous system: ADHD attention governed by interest, challenge, novelty, urgency, passion.
- **Kasatskii et al./JetBrains (2023):** High perceptual load degrades ADHD-symptomatic developers' performance faster.
- **Mark et al. (2024):** Context-switching costs approach an hour for ADHD developers (ICSE).
- **Newman & Begel (2025):** 10.6% of programmers have ADHD (ICSE 2025).
- **Eisenberg et al. (2008):** DRD4/7R allele shows opposite fitness outcomes by environment. ADHD traits are adaptive in the right context.
- **Clark & Chalmers (1998):** Extended Mind Thesis. Cognitive tools are genuine extensions of the mind, not mere aids. The theoretical foundation for design-as-prosthesis.
